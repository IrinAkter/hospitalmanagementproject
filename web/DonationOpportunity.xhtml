  • Opportunities for Giving

Our staff is prepared to advise donors who wish to contribute their support. There are many ways to give and we welcome the opportunity to suggest how charitable contributions can have both an immediate impact and lasting benefit for both the hospital and the donor.

Outright Gift / Donate Now

This is the most popular form of giving to support the programs and services at Milford Hospital. All contributions are tax deductible to the extent allowed by law and may be unrestricted and used to support the entire mission of the hospital or may be restricted for a specific project, program or hospital service.

There are many types of outright gifts including:

Securities

Donors may also choose to make gifts of stocks, bonds or the revenue generated from the sale of real estate. There can be a substantial tax advantage for utilizing your appreciated securities to support Milford Hospital. You will pay no taxes on the gain in shares that you donate, plus you will be able to deduct the fully appreciated amount of your shares as a charitable contribution to the Milford Hospital Foundation.

Matching Gifts

Many companies will match their employees and retirees contributions (time and dollars) to Milford Hospital. Please contact your employer / former employer (usually Human Resources) to obtain the appropriate paperwork to process a matching gift to Milford Hospital.

Real Estate

This is a wonderful way for a donor to make a significant contribution. A gift of real estate will enable you to have major capital gains and tax benefits while at the same time helping Milford Hospital. Property that has been held for a long time makes an excellent charitable gift and gives you many advantages.

Should you have any questions or require additional information, we can be reached at 203.647.7011. Or, to make an immediate donation, you may:

  • Call the Milford Hospital Foundation at 203.647.7011
  • Mail to: The Milford Hospital Foundation, 300 Seaside Avenue, Milford, CT 06460

Milford Hospital Annual Fund

The Milford Hospital Foundation appeals directly to the community, our staff and our physicians for contributions to our Annual Fund. Because these contributions are donated without restriction, the Hospital's Senior Management is able to apply them where they are most needed throughout the year. Annual fund gifts provide essential funding to all areas of the hospital that are not funded by other sources.

Tribute and Honor Gifts

Tribute gifts to the Milford Hospital Foundation are a meaningful way to remember, honor and recognize an individual or an occasion. This type of gift is often made to acknowledge:

  • A wedding, birth or milestone
  • Friends, family and loved ones
  • Physicians, Nurses or care-givers
  • Appreciation for kindness and service

The Milford Hospital Foundation will acknowledge your gift by sending a letter to the person whom the honor / tribute was made for. The amount of the gift will not be disclosed and a separate letter of thanks will be sent to you. To make a donation, you may:

  • Call the Milford Hospital Foundation at 203.647.7011
  • Mail to: The Milford Hospital Foundation, 300 Seaside Avenue, Milford, CT 06460

** In the NOTES box, be sure to name the person and /or occasion you would like to honor. Include their name and address so that we may send them an acknowledgement letter.

Gifts in Remembrance / Memorial Gifts

Memorial Gifts offer tribute to exceptional people you wish to remember and ensure a lasting legacy for your loved ones. Memorial Gifts can be unrestricted or you may opt to designate your gift to a specific program or project funded by the Foundation including the Fairway Suite / Hospice Room at Milford Hospital (link to Fairway Suite)

The Milford Hospital Foundation will acknowledge your gift by sending a letter to the family of the person you wish to memorialize. The amount of the gift will not be disclosed and a separate letter of thanks will be sent to you.

To make a donation, you may:

  • Call the Milford Hospital Foundation at 203.647.7011
  • Mail to: The Milford Hospital Foundation, 300 Seaside Avenue, Milford, CT 06460

** In the NOTES box, please indicate that this is a Memorial Gift and name the person you wish to remember. Also include the name and address of the family member to whom you would like us to send an acknowledgement letter.

Fairway Suite

The Milford Hospital "Fairway Suite", is an acute, hospice care inpatient room. It was made possible by the generous donation of Carol McInnis and her family in honor of her husband, Robert who passed away in October, 2005. It is supported by the continuing fund-raising efforts of the Milford Hospital Foundation.

The Fairway Suite offers a home-like bedroom with adjoining family room specifically designed for the care and comfort of hospice patients and their families. Through our partnership with local VNA's, Milford Hospital provides personalized services in a caring environment and is committed to keeping our patients in their community.

To make a donation to The Fairway Suite you may:

  • Call the Milford Hospital Foundation at 203.647.7011
  • Mail to: The Milford Hospital Foundation, 300 Seaside Avenue, Milford, CT 06460

** In the notes box, be sure to indicate the donation is being made to The Fairway Suite. If you wish to honor or remember someone with a gift to The Fairway Suite, please include the name and address of whom to send an acknowledgement letter.

Planned Gifts

Planned giving is among the most important way donors may help to ensure the vitality of Milford Hospital for future generations. A planned gift may include a bequest in one's will, a trust arrangement, life income gifts, or gifts of life insurance. Planned gifts may be restricted for special purposes such as capital expansion, building and equipment purchases and also may be negotiated to "endow" the operations of specific hospital departments.

Wills and Bequests

You may decide to leave some part or all of your estate to the Milford Hospital Foundation. Bequests are an excellent option for those who want to pledge their support for Milford Hospital today, but cannot part with substantial assets during their lifetime.

Life Income Gifts

The Foundation encourages you to speak with your financial advisor about incomeproducing gifts such as Deferred Gift Annuities, Charitable Remainder Trusts, Charitable Remainder.

Annuities and Retained Life Estates

You may name Milford Hospital as a partial or sole beneficiary, of your life insurance policy. This is an ideal way to re-assign a policy that is no longer needed by you or your spouse, or that names beneficiaries that no longer require the future income.

If you would like more information on how you can help Milford Hospital in the future or in including Milford Hospital with this type of legacy gift, please contact the Milford Hospital Foundation at 203.647.7011. We would be happy to meet with you in person to discuss your personal areas of interest.

Major Gifts

Individuals and families may invest in Milford Hospital's strategic plans to initiate new services and programs, modernize our facilities and infrastructure, advance our technological capabilities, purchase equipment and renovate or construct new facilities.

Opportunities are numerous to permanently recognize donors whose generosity permits any of these visionary steps to secure Milford Hospital's future. We are eager to meet with potential donors to explore your philanthropic interests and how they align with the hospital's needs. Please call the Milford Hospital Foundation for more information: 203.647.7011.

Tax Benefits of Donating

What are the tax benefits of contributing to the Milford Hospital Foundation? The Milford Hospital Foundation is recognized by the Internal Revenue Service as exempt from paying federal income taxes under section 501 (c) (3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

You may be eligible for various tax benefits such as income tax and estate tax deductions as well as reduced capital gains tax liability. Tax laws may vary by location and are always subject to change. Please consult a certified public accountant or certified financial planner for details on how your taxes will be impacted by your gift.
