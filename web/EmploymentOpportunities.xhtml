Case Manager

Will be responsible for providing verbal and written explanation of observation to all observation status admissions within 24 hours of admission.  Will also be responsible for facilitating appropriate level of care assignment and discharge planning in the Emergency Department.  Also, ensure that anticipated discharges on the Medical Surgical units occur.  Qualifications:  RN with a BSN and Connecticut State License, as well as 3-5 years of Case Management experience.  Knowledge of Milliman Care Guidelines preferred; Utilization Review and Discharge Planning experience in an acute hospital care setting; knowledge of Medicare guidelines; computer skills required. 

(2) 20 hrs./wk.- day shift positions available to include every other weekend.

(2) Per Diem positions to cover hours as needed within the Dept.  No guarantee of weekly hours worked/no benefits with this position. 

 

Central Supply/Distribution -Manager

40 hrs. /wk. on the day shift.  Will manage the Distribution staff responsible for the stocking and delivery of supplies to all areas of the Hospital.  Will oversee Central Supply, Linen, Stockroom & Loading Dock personnel and all required functions.  Will provide timely reports to the Director of Materials Management.  Will interface and cooperate with Department Heads to determine supply stocking requirements and delivery schedules. Will be the Materials Management Department designee for several Hospital committees.  Required education to include a High School diploma and having at least 3 years related experience preferably in a healthcare environment.  A Bachelor's degree in Business Administration or related field preferred.  Demonstrated competencies in analyzing detailed information along with excellent communication and leadership skills required.  Prior supervisory experience preferred.  Solid computer skills including Microsoft Office functions is required.  Meditech experience preferred. 

Certified Nursing Assistant

Performs various duties assisting in the treatment and nursing care for the pediatric, adolescent, adult and geriatric patient based on a fundamental knowledge and utilization of the nursing process and in accordance with established hospital policies and procedures.  Qualifications:  Must be a graduate of a Certified Nursing Assistant Program; State of Connecticut Certification is required. Prior acute care experience preferred. Skills:  BLS; basic computer skills including the ability to enter information pertaining to patient care per hospital policy.  Familiarity in working in Windows environment; knowledge of medical terminology; demonstrated working knowledge of a computerized medical information management system; demonstrated positive interpersonal skills; demonstrated ability to communicate effectively and project a positive image; good written and verbal communication skills required.

Certified Surgical Technician - OR

The Surgical Technologist is an individual with specialized education who functions as a member of the surgical team in the role of the scrub person.  He / she handles the instruments, supplies and equipment necessary during surgical procedures.  Will have an understanding of the procedure being performed and anticipate the needs of the surgeon.  Will have the necessary knowledge and ability to ensure quality patient care during the operative procedure and is constantly on vigil for maintenance of the sterile field.  Surgical Technologists work under medical supervision to facilitate the safe and effective conduct of invasive surgical procedures.  Qualifications: Must be a Certified Surgical Technologist, a graduate of an accredited Surgical Technologist program;  at least 2 years of prior experience is preferred; current  BLS Certification required.

Full Time 40 hours, flexible schedule Monday - Friday, 7:00 AM - 5:30 PM, Call rotation for weekdays, weekends and Holidays is required.

Part Time 20 hours,  flexible schedule Monday - Friday, 7:00 AM - 5:30 PM, Call rotation for weekdays, weekends and Holidays is required.

 

Coder (Inpatient)

Responsible for identifying principal and secondary diagnoses and procedures on all Inpatient discharges, assigning appropriate ICD-10 and PCS codes to each and for verfiying and maintaining the accuracy of the abstracted clinical and financial data, in an acute care Hospital environment.  Accepts accountability for quality, timeliness, completeness and accuracy of inpatient coding and all abstraction and data entry to support all regulatory compliance, including core measures and hospital statistics.  Qualifications:  Formal HIM education with National Certification AHIMA-CCS, (RHIA, RHIT). Miminum 2 years progressive coding or coding review experience in inpatient coding with claims processing and/or data management responisbilities.  Strong ICD-10 and PCS coding skills and understanding of coding ethics and compliance.  Strong computer PC/skills.

40 hrs./wk. full time (will consider 24 hrs./wk. part time)

Cook

Director - Pharmacy

Full time day position.  Responsibilities:  Oversees and directs day-to-day operations for Pharmacy services.  The director of pharmacy position is responsible for the operation and management of the Pharmacy Department.  In addition, the incumbent will plan, organize and implement hospital pharmacy policies  and procedures in accordance with established policies of the hospital, in cooperation with the other departments of the hospital, and in accordance with accepted standards of good pharmacy practice.  Requirements:  Current registration as a registered pharmacist in Connecticut.  Advanced degree in pharmacy and prior experience as a director, manager or assistant manager in a Hospital pharmacy; or graduation from an accredited school of pharmacy with demonstrated experience as a director, manager or assistant manager in a hospital pharmacy.
